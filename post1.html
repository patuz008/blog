CAUSES OF CONFLICT IN RELATIONSHIPS

Relationships, whether romantic or platonic are peculiar experiences.

Posted by Admin on September 01, 2021

Relationships, whether romantic or platonic are peculiar experiences. As humans, we are social beings and must associate with other humans after all no man is an island. Therefore social; interaction is inevitable.

But it is also imperative to know when your social interactions and relationships are incompatible as it can cause certain emotional scars and mental stress.

Social Avoidance, isolating oneself from society and social interactions can be a big hindering factor, socially avoidant persons find it hard to understand what people want, need or maneuver socially because as with everything in life we have to constantly refine our social skills to avoid social awkwardness. This is applicable to interpersonal and romantic relationships.

The following are reasons for frictions in social interactions and relationships:

  1. Miscommunication: You don’t communicate effectively, this could manifest in one’s inability to express themselves whether through body language or verbally, also inability to pick up on non-verbal cues, tone of voice etc. all these cause disagreements and resentments.
  2. Too much polarity: when individuals are too different in ideologies, hobbies and area of interest. This means the similarities are too scarce or even nonexistent.
  3. Inability to adapt or grow: this also stems from lack of understanding of what makes the friendship thrive. Individuals or couples should instead grow, evolve together, knowing what each other wants, likes and dislikes. Giving each other the motivation to be better and express one's true self.
  4. Avoidance of Issues to attain temporary peace: there is a need for honest open communication. Your opinion should be aired even if it is not in agreement with the other person. Criticism should be done in a peaceful, constructive and sensitive manner. Conflicts can be positive bonding experiences for growth and deeper mutual understanding.
  5. No attraction or likeness: many romantic relationships are plagued with lack of romance, they don’t spend time together. According to Robert Sternberg in his book titled The Triangular theory of love, list three things in romantic relationships [Intimacy, Passion and Commitment].

Finally, there are many other factors that cause conflicts in relationships and friendships such as lack of trust, lying, bad attitude, selfishness and self-centeredness, indifference, lack of effort etc. As we should know, no one or relationship is perfect. It just takes time, dedication and constant effort to grow in understanding of what our partner, friends, or loved ones want and need. And in Romantic relationships, find who is worth the effort.
